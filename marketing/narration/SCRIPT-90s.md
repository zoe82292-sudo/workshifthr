Say **“ShiftWorks HR”** as two words. Pause ~½ second between sections.  
Target: **~90 seconds** (about 215–230 words). Steady pace — don’t rush the open.

Save as `Voice to us - Zoe.m4a` on Desktop when done.

**[intro · ~22s]**  
If you’ve ever spent days in Excel before merit week — or anytime leadership asks who’s underpaid — you know the fear: you might still miss something. I built ShiftWorks HR for that moment. Upload your compensation census and in under thirty seconds you get who needs attention, what it’ll cost to fix, and a summary leadership can actually use.

**[upload · ~10s]**  
You already have the file — Workday, ADP, UKG, or a spreadsheet. Drop it in. No new HRIS. Nothing for IT to configure.

**[dashboard · ~10s]**  
First: a prioritized list of what to fix before letters go out — not fifty tabs. Critical items rise to the top so you know where to start.

**[budget · ~12s]**  
Then the number finance will ask for: cost to bring people up to range, what’s in the merit pool, and your total exposure. So you’re not guessing in the meeting.

**[issues · ~10s]**  
Every flag is a real person — name, department, and the exact dollar gap. Act on it the same day instead of rebuilding another spreadsheet.

**[managers · ~10s]**  
It also catches the awkward ones — a manager earning less than someone they lead — so you can handle it before it becomes a surprise.

**[equity · ~8s]**  
When leadership asks about pay fairness, you can show median pay by gender and race from your own data — ready for the conversation.

**[pdf · ~6s]**  
Need to brief executives? One click — risk, exposure, and the talking points they’ll ask about.

**[cta · ~5s]**
