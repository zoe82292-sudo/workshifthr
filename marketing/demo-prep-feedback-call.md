## 1. Product in one breath (memorize this)

**Name:** ShiftWorksHR  

**What it is:**  
A focused **compensation analysis** tool for anytime you have a roster/census and need answers fast. Comp and HR teams upload an export from Workday, UKG, ADP, or Excel/CSV and get a **severity-ranked review queue**, dollar gaps, budget exposure, pay equity signals, and a **leadership PDF** — usually in under a minute.

**When people use it (not just merit):**
- Range / grade structure reviews  
- Mid-year or off-cycle compensation checks  
- Before promotions or re-orgs  
- After a big hiring wave (new-hire placement vs ranges)  
- When leadership asks about pay fairness or “who’s underpaid”  
- Merit / annual review season (common, but not the only moment)

**What it is not:**
- Not an ATS / recruiting platform  
- Not payroll  
- Not a full HRIS or configured compensation *system*  
- Not a legal pay-equity audit  

**How to say it:**  
> “Anytime a mid-size HR or comp team is staring at a compensation spreadsheet and needs to know who’s out of range, what’s inverted, and what it’ll cost to fix — without waiting on an IT project to configure another platform.”

## 3. Why buy this instead of “just configure the platform”

This is the heart of the story. Say it out loud.

**The false choice people hear:**  
Configure Workday Advanced Comp / a big suite **or** stay in Excel forever.

**The reality you lived:**  
Even mid-size companies with a serious HRIS still end up in exports and spreadsheets for range QA, inversions, and “what does finance need by Friday?” They’re past “wing it in a Google Sheet with no ranges,” but not yet in “fully configured Advanced Comp with a dedicated team.” Configuration projects take months, need IT, and still don’t replace the messy first-pass review people do by hand.

**Why ShiftWorksHR instead of configuration:**

| Configure a platform | Use ShiftWorksHR |
| --- | --- |
| Months + IT + vendor | Upload today |
| Expensive, org-wide project | $249/cycle (or annual if year-round) |
| Deep system of record | Lightweight QA on the export you already have |
| Great *eventually* | Useful *this week* when leadership asks |

**Line to use:**  
> “I’m not anti-platform. Platforms are the system of record. I’m anti-waiting — and anti-rebuilding the same Excel model every time someone asks who’s below minimum. ShiftWorksHR sits on the export you already pull. No configuration project.”

## 9. Challenging questions — answers

### Positioning

**“Who is this for — SMBs? Enterprise?”**  
> “Mid-size first — roughly 150 to a couple thousand employees. Big enough that ranges and inversions matter; small enough that they often don’t have a configured compensation suite or a big analyst team. That’s the Excel gap.”

**“Is this only for merit season?”**  
> “Merit is when the pain spikes, but the same QA matters for range reviews, mid-year checks, promotions, post-hiring waves, and anytime leadership asks who’s out of range. Pricing is often per cycle because that’s how budgets work — usage isn’t limited to ‘merit week only.’”

**“Is this an HRIS / recruiting / payroll tool?”**  
> “No. Compensation analysis on a census export. We don’t replace those systems.”

**“Why not just configure Workday / UKG Advanced Comp?”**  
> “Do both if you have the budget and time — platforms are the long-term system. But configuration is an IT project. I built this for the gap: you already export the file; you need answers this week without waiting for a rollout. Upload, don’t configure.”

**“How is this different from Excel? / This can all be done in Excel.”**  
> “You’re right — it *can*. That’s what I used to do.  
>  
> The issue isn’t that Excel can’t answer it — it’s that doing it *well* takes a long time, and it’s easy to miss something before a leadership meeting. For a mid-size census, a clean pass was **days** of work for me. If the file was messy or I got interrupted, it could stretch toward a **week**.  
>  
> Would you agree that’s usually not a quick afternoon if you want every check and something finance can actually read?  
>  
> ShiftWorksHR is that same checklist, already built — upload, prioritized queue and PDF in under a minute. You spend time deciding, not rebuilding formulas. I’m not replacing Excel as a working file — I’m replacing the days of archaeology before the meeting.”

**“How is this different from market-pricing tools?”**  
> “Those help set or benchmark ranges. We QA the census *against* the ranges you already have.”

### Trust & early stage

**“Why would anyone upload salaries / sensitive comp data?”**  
> “Fair question — it’s sensitive. A few things that help:  
>  
> Files are processed **in memory** for the analysis. We don’t keep the spreadsheet by default. Saving to history is opt-in. Exports (PDF/Excel) generate in the browser onto their device. Traffic is **HTTPS**. We don’t train ML models on uploads.  
>  
> For a first look, people use the **public sample** (fictional data) or upload a **sanitized file** — Employee ID, salary, ranges, no names if they prefer. There’s a security summary and DPA on the site for IT/procurement.  
>  
> I’m honest we’re early. Trust is earned with clear handling, not buzzwords.”

**“Where does the data go? Do you store it?”**  
> “Default: no. Upload → analyze in memory → results back to the browser → in-memory copy discarded. We don’t run a warehouse of employee salary rows.  
>  
> If someone clicks **Save to history**, a JSON snapshot of results can be stored for their signed-in org account (limited runs), and they can delete it. That’s optional.”

**“Who can see our file?”**  
> “Only their authorized signed-in users for that organization. Sharing login outside the authorized team isn’t allowed. I recommend they limit access to comp/HR staff and sign out when done.”

**“Are you SOC 2 / ISO / HIPAA certified?”**  
> “Not today — don’t invent that. Here’s what we *do* publish: security summary, privacy policy, DPA for procurement. Happy to walk IT through the page. Many mid-size teams start with the sample or a sanitized census while they evaluate.”

**“What about GDPR / employee privacy?”**  
> “The customer remains responsible for being authorized to upload and for how they use demographic fields. If gender/race are in the file, we only use them for descriptive comparisons, hide small groups (&lt;5), and don’t keep those fields long-term unless they save history. Point them to privacy + security pages.”

**“Can we try without real data?”**  
> “Yes — that’s what sample-preview is for. Fictional sample only. Or sanitize: strip names, keep IDs and ranges.”
